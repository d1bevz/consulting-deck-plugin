# Consulting Presentation Principles

## Core Framework: SCR (Situation -> Complication -> Resolution)

### Structure
- **Introduction (5-10%):** Situation + Complication -- why should the audience listen?
- **Main Body (70-80%):** Resolution -- main message + supporting arguments
- **Conclusion (5-15%):** Call to action -- what now?

### Minto Pyramid
- **Main Message:** Short, self-contained, answers the key question, is a synthesis (not a list), is new to the audience
- **3-4 Arguments:** Answer "why?" or "how?", MECE (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive)
- **Supporting Logic:** Inductive or deductive, same type and weight per argument

## Horizontal Logic
- Slide titles form a coherent narrative when read in sequence
- Each title is a **conclusion** (not a description)
- Test: read only the titles -- does the story make sense?

## Vertical Logic
- One slide = one message
- Title states the conclusion
- Body provides 2-3 supporting arguments/evidence
- Everything on the slide supports the title

## Three Perception Levels

### Presentation Level
| Level | Time | What |
|-------|------|------|
| Conclusions | 30 sec | Executive summary |
| Logic & arguments | 5 min | Key slides, main logic |
| Details | 40 min | Full read, reproduce arguments |

### Slide Level
| Level | Time | What |
|-------|------|------|
| Title & key insight | 5 sec | Read/don't read decision |
| Overall logic | 15 sec | Agree/disagree |
| Details | 2 min | Form own conclusions, questions |

## Slide Content Guidelines
- **Title:** Max 12-15 words, max 2 lines, bold 22-24pt
- **Body:** 80-140 words including subtitles, callouts, chart labels
- **Total text equivalent:** ~200-250 words per slide
- **Source:** Always present, bottom-left, min 9pt

## Typography Rules
- Main font: min 12pt
- Footnotes/sources: min 9pt
- Line length: 30-40 characters
- No indented first lines
- Paragraphs: max 4-5 lines
- Paragraph spacing: 50% of font height
- Single line spacing
- If text doesn't fit: **shorten the text**, not the font

## Closing Options
- What are the specific next steps? (decide, approve, hire, ...)
- What benefit will the audience gain by acting?
- What did the audience not know before this presentation?
- What risk does the audience face by not following the recommendations?
